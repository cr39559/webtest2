Top 10 Frequently Asked Questions

Can you liquid cool my PC?


Yes, I can liquid cool you PC, however consider that maintenece should be performed at least every 6 months. Also consider the extra cost and little performance gain from liquid cooling a system. Alternatively, you can liquid cool a CPU with an AIO (All in One) cooler and some graphics cards come with a pre attached AIO that do not require maintenence. Note, liquid cooled components will eventually permeate and will need to be replaced once the liquid has evaporated (Usually after 8+ years).

Will my computer be Windows 11 ready?


As long as the CPU and the motherboard supports it, your PC should be Windows 11 ready. Most new CPUs and motherboards will support windows 11.

Can you build with used parts?


As long as the used parts are bought by the customer, yes I can build with used parts. This will however void any warranty associated with the build as well as refunds.

Will I get a warranty with my PC?


Yes, you will get a limited warranty (30 days) as most issues can be caught within a couple of days of the PC being powered on. You may contact me to diagnose the issue and replace any faulty components free of charge. Alternatively, a partial refund may be requested at any time before the 30 days are up. Please consider the following before asking for a refund; individual components may carry longer warranties from their respective manufacturers. For example, some Seasonic power supplies carry up to a 10 year warranty.

Can you install Linux on my PC?


Yes, I can install linux on your PC as well as other operating systems.

Why does my PC not display to my monitor?


Sometimes, your PC may not display to a monitor. Please make sure A) you have plugged in the cable to the correct VGA port and B) that your monitor is turned on/correct input selected and C) that you restart the computer by holding the power button for a few seconds and turn it back on by pressing the power buttom. If all of those steps fail, Please feel free to contact me for assistance, or try looking up solutions yourself.

Can you overclock my PC?


Yes, I can overclock your components, but it will void the manufacturer warranty for each part that's overclocked. Additionally if a PC is overclocked, I cannot guarantee system stability as overclocking is outside of guaranteed system spec. You can contact me to return components to system rated spec if they become unstable for a small fee.

Is my PC upgradable?


Yes, almost all custom PCs are upgradable. You can either upgrade them yourself or have me do it for you for a small fee.

What if I dont want the PC anymore?


Unfortunately, if you no longer want the PC after the parts have been purchased either A) You can keep the parts or B) I may buy the parts if I have a use for them.

Do you have payment plans?


While I do not have payment plans right now, you can order a PC part by part until you have a full system.
